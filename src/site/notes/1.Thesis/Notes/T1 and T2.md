### Relaxation time mechanisms
>The time difference between T1 and T2 is extremely important in all MR imaging or spectroscopy. We always use a repeated sequence of RF and gradient pulses, with a repetition time (TR).

> T1- weighted images highlight fat and provide detailed anatomical information, while T2-weighted images emphasise water content , making them useful for detecting abnormalities like inflammation and edema

#### $T_1$: relaxation
- [Also known as spin-lattice relaxation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spin%E2%80%93lattice_relaxation) or transverse magnetisation
- Nuclei decreasing their megnetisation in the transverse plane 
- The spins can **transfer energy** to the surrounding lattice, allowing them to relax back to equilibrium
- [T1 is the relaxation time for the recovery of magnetization along the z axis](/img/user/1.Thesis/Resources/Books/MRI-FromPictureToProton.pdf)
- It is the time it takes for the longitudinal magnetization to recover approximately 63% of its initial value after being flipped into the magnetic transverse plane by a 90° radiofrequency pulse
- [Measuring T_1: ](/img/user/1.Thesis/Resources/Books/MRI-FromPictureToProton.pdf)

#### $T_2$: decay/delay
- Also known as decay of longitudinal magnetisation
- Or process of nuclei increasion their magentisation in the longitudinal plane (parallel to z)
- Measuring $T_2$: [Measuring T](/img/user/1.Thesis/Resources/Books/MRI-FromPictureToProton.pdf)
- **No energy is actually lost** from the spin system but the decay of transverse magnetization arises from the loss of phase coherence between spins, which arises from magnetic field inhomogeneities
